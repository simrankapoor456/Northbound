\---



Northbound was never about building another piece of software.



It was never about creating another job board.



It was never about helping people send more applications.



It was always about people.



\---



I genuinely care about people.



I love relationships.



I love building trust.



I love creating value.



I love helping others.



There is something incredibly fulfilling about knowing you made someone's life just a little bit easier.



Not because it makes me feel superior.



Quite the opposite.



Because it reminds me how fortunate I've been.



Fortunate to receive an education.



Fortunate to have a family that believed in me.



Fortunate to have friends who supported me.



Fortunate to have mentors who gave me their time.



Every time I'm able to help someone, it reminds me that I was once the person who needed help too.



That feeling never gets old.



\---



I also want to become someone people can rely on.



Someone people trust.



Someone people remember.



Someone teammates genuinely enjoy working with.



Someone great teams are excited to build with.



Not because I'm the smartest person in the room.



But because I care.



Because I show up.



Because I make the people around me better.



Because I leave things a little better than I found them.



That's the kind of engineer I want to become.



More importantly...



That's the kind of person I want to become.



\---



Growing up, my dad and my uncle always told me:



> "Connections are more powerful than hard work."



For a long time...



I didn't really understand what they meant.



I thought working harder would always be enough.



Sometimes it is.



Sometimes it isn't.



As I got older, I realized something else.



I wasn't very good at maintaining relationships.



I'd meet someone.



We'd have great conversations.



Maybe we'd stay in touch for a little while.



Then life would happen.



School.



Work.



Projects.



Stress.



Time would pass.



Eventually I'd think about reaching back out...



Only because I needed something.



Maybe a referral.



Maybe advice.



Maybe a reference.



And every single time...



I hated that feeling.



Not because I needed help.



But because I realized I hadn't invested in the relationship.



Relationships aren't something you build only when you need them.



They're something you nurture long before you ever ask for anything.



That's the kind of person I want to become.



Someone who stays in touch.



Someone who celebrates other people's wins.



Someone who checks in without needing a reason.



Someone who builds relationships that last years.



Maybe even a lifetime.



Relationships that help you find...



A mentor.



A teammate.



A best friend.



A co-founder.



A hiring manager who believes in you.



A person you can always turn to.



And just as importantly...



Someone who knows they can always turn to you.



\---



Hard work matters.



Always.



But relationships create opportunities that hard work alone sometimes never can.



The world isn't always fair.



Sometimes the biggest opportunity in your life begins because one person believed in you before your resume ever reaches a recruiter.



Instead of complaining about that reality...



I want to help people navigate it.



The right way.



Not through spam.



Not through fake networking.



Not through transactional relationships.



Not through desperation.



But through genuine curiosity.



Kindness.



Consistency.



Authenticity.



And meaningful relationships.



People may not remember every project you built.



But they'll remember how you made them feel while building it together.



\---



I don't want people to become better applicants.



I want them to become remarkable builders.



The kind of people others naturally want on their team.



The kind of engineers people trust.



The kind of teammates people rely on.



The kind of builders who inspire others to keep learning.



The kind of people who make everyone around them a little better.



Because that's the kind of person I want to become too.
